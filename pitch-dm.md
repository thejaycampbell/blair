# Blair -- DM to Apple CMO

You reviewed Blair. You gave it a real brief.
Here is what changed.

---

The three things you said would make it real:

**1. A feedback loop.**
Blair now logs every correction you make to a persistent learnings file.
Every specialist reads it before producing any output.
You tell it once. It applies it everywhere, every session, going forward.
The tool gets more useful the longer you run it.
That is what you said was missing. That is what changed.

**2. Proactive agenda.**
/blair:brief is now a morning skill.
It reads what is live, what is due, what is drifting, and what competitors published in the last 7 days.
You open it before the first meeting. It tells you what to pay attention to.
No prompt required.

**3. The lock that actually locks.**
Marquee Messaging is now enforced, not just documented.
Every specialist reads it before delivering. Drift gets caught before it ships.
You set the messaging once. Every output after that is checked against it.

---

Two more things you named:

**Stakeholder context.**
There is now a structured file for relationship dynamics.
You write who matters and why -- the Singapore partner going through a transition,
the agency lead who hates long emails, the exec who is still sensitive about Q3.
Blair reads it every session and adjusts tone accordingly.
You fill it in. Blair uses it.

**Draft to send.**
Email drafts now connect to Gmail. You approve. Blair sends.
GTM timelines become actual calendar blocks.

---

What we did not solve:

Relationship intuition takes time to build.
Blair can use what you give it.
It cannot observe what you do not record.
That is honest.

---

One ask.

Not a demo. Not a trial account.

Give us a real problem you are working on right now.
A campaign you are about to brief. A positioning question that is not settled.
A launch that needs a story.

Run Blair against it.
If the output is not materially better than what you reviewed, you owe us nothing.
If it is, we would like to talk about what Apple would need to make this something your team actually uses.

One session. Real brief. No slides.
